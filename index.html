The Bioscience Context

In oil palm agronomy, harvesting a Fresh Fruit Bunch (FFB) at the precise biological window is critical. Harvesting too early cuts your oil extraction rate (OER) drastically, while waiting too long triggers fruit detachment and spikelet rot. By calculating the **derivative $f'(x)$**, agronomists can step away from visual guesswork and map exactly when a plantation block enters its peak daily mass-accumulation sprint.

Mathematical Framework

Accumulated Weight Function $f(x)$:

f(x) = L / (1 + e^(-k * (x - x0)))

Instantaneous Daily Growth Rate $f'(x)$:

f'(x) = k * f(x) * (1 - (f(x) / L))
